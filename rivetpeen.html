Let's Peen a Rivet

It's just a permanent metal fastener used in medieval armour, railway bridges and your car. The humble rivet is very strong yet it can be the very functional asthetic your piece needs. Pick out your flavor of sheet metal and let's peen some rivets!!

(clockwise L to R) Wire Cutter, Metal Shears, Anvil, Sandpaper,
Ball-Peen Hammer, Gloves, Roofing Nails, and Sheet Metal

Use the metal shears to cut out sheets of metal. If it's hard to cut, get a pair of stronger shears or use thinner sheet metal.

Finished cut metal and roofing nails. Smooth the sharp metal edges of your piece with some sandpaper or a metal file.

Use a metal punch/hammer to put a hole in the sheet metal. You can also create a pilot hole with a drill.

Tap the roofing nail through the pilot hole in the sheet metal and place on your anvil.

Put the nail through the hole and
cut off the end with the wire cutters.

Anvil time! Hit the nail with a "dead blow" to flatten the nail's top.

The flat nail top. Notice the nail has buldged and locked into position.

Using the round hammer head side, peen the nail's flat top in a circular strike pattern.

Your finished peened rivet! Take this skill to a new level. What will you make next?
